# Cognitive Linguistics — Literature Map

**Status:** Outline. Ready for population by reviewer.

## Core question
How do humans construct meaning, and how does SRE's model relate to
established theories of meaning in cognitive linguistics?

## Current references
- [16] Fillmore (1982) — Frame Semantics
- [17] Lakoff (1987) — Women, Fire, and Dangerous Things
- [18] Langacker (1987) — Foundations of Cognitive Grammar
- [19] Talmy (2000) — Toward a Cognitive Semantics

## Gaps / challenges
- SRE does not model grammatical constructions (yet).
- SRE's category system is definitional, not prototype-based — tension
  with Lakoff.
- Affordances are static property lists, not dynamic force-dynamic
  patterns — tension with Talmy.
- SRE assumes meaning can be fully specified in advance; usage-based
  theory argues meaning emerges from use — fundamental tension with
  Tomasello.

## Research lineage note
> SRE's notion of affordances is directly inherited from Gibson's
> ecological psychology (1979). Its semantic representation model is
> compatible with Fillmore's frame semantics and Langacker's symbolic
> units. However, its claim that meaning can be fully specified prior
> to language use is in tension with usage-based and emergentist
> approaches (Tomasello, Hopper). This tension is acknowledged but not
> resolved in the current architecture.
