## Experience 1: Te whakareri — Before the Journey

**Scenario:** At home in South Africa, packing. Thinking about the trip ahead.

**Entities:** PERSON (traveller), PLACE (Aotearoa), THING (taonga/gift), ACTION (haere/go), STATE (excited)

**Affordances:**
- traveller packs gift
- traveller prepares to travel

**Phrases:**
| Priority | Phrase | English | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| MUST SAY | Kei te haere au ki Aotearoa | I am going to New Zealand | unverified |
| RECOGNIZE | He taonga tēnei mō te whānau | This is a gift for the family | unverified |
| RECOGNIZE | Kei te koa au | I am excited/happy | unverified |

**Interaction rehearsal:**
1. Your wife says: "Kei te haere au ki Aotearoa."
2. Someone asks: "Kei te pēhea koe?" → She answers: "Kei te koa au."
3. She shows the gift: "He taonga tēnei."

**Content:**
```
"Kei te haere au ki Aotearoa. He taonga tēnei mō te whānau.
Kei te koa au ki te kite i a rātou."
```

## Experience 2: Te taenga — Arrival

**Scenario:** Arriving at the airport. Being greeted by family.

**Entities:** PERSON (traveller), PERSON (whaea/mother), PERSON (matua/father), PERSON (whānau/family), ACTION (greet), STATE (happy)

**Affordances:**
- family greets traveller
- traveller greets family

**Phrases:**
| Priority | Phrase | English | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| MUST SAY | Kia ora | Hello / greetings | verified |
| MUST SAY | Kei te pēhea koe? | How are you? | verified |
| MUST SAY | Kei te pai | I am well | verified |
| RECOGNIZE | Haere mai | Welcome / come here | verified |

**Interaction rehearsal:**
1. Family member says: "Kia ora! Haere mai!"
2. They ask: "Kei te pēhea koe?" → She says: "Kei te pai."
3. She asks them: "Kei te pēhea kōrua?" (How are you two?)

**Content:**
```
"Kia ora! Haere mai ki Aotearoa.
Kei te pēhea koe? Kei te pai au, e koa ana au ki te kite i a koutou."
```

## Experience 3: Te whakataki whānau — Meeting the Family

**Scenario:** Being introduced to family members at the house.

**Entities:** PERSON (traveller), PERSON (whaea), PERSON (matua), PERSON (kuia/grandmother), PERSON (koroua/grandfather), PERSON (tamariki/children), ACTION (introduce), ACTION (awhi/hug)

**Affordances:**
- traveller meets family
- family welcomes with hongi (pressing noses)

**Phrases:**
| Priority | Phrase | English | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| MUST SAY | Ko ___ tōku ingoa | My name is ___ | unverified |
| MUST SAY | Nō hea koe? | Where are you from? | verified |
| MUST SAY | Nō Āwherika ki te Tonga ahau | I am from South Africa | unverified |
| RECOGNIZE | Ko wai tēnei? | Who is this? | verified |
| CULTURAL | Tēnā koutou | Greetings to you all (formal) | verified |
| CULTURAL | Hongi | Press noses (action, not word) | — |

**Interaction rehearsal:**
1. Family member says: "Ko wai tēnei?" ("Ko whaea Pita tēnei.")
2. They ask: "Nō hea koe?" → She says: "Nō Āwherika ki te Tonga ahau."
3. She introduces herself: "Ko ___ tōku ingoa."
4. Practice hongi greeting (cultural gesture).

**Content:**
```
"Ko wai tēnei? Ko whaea ____ tēnei.
Nō hea koe? Nō Āwherika ki te Tonga ahau.
Tēnā koutou e te whānau."
```

## Experience 4: Te whare — At the House

**Scenario:** Being shown around the family home.

**Entities:** THING (whare/house), THING (moenga/bed), PLACE (wharepaku/bathroom), PLACE (kāuta/kitchen), ACTION (show), ACTION (noho/sit), STATE (hiamoe/sleepy)

**Affordances:**
- family shows house
- traveller sits
- traveller sleeps

**Phrases:**
| Priority | Phrase | English | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| MUST SAY | Kei hea te wharepaku? | Where is the bathroom? | verified |
| RECOGNIZE | He whare ātaahua | A beautiful house | unverified |
| RECOGNIZE | Noho mai | Please sit / stay | verified |
| RECOGNIZE | Kei te hiamoe au | I am sleepy | unverified |

**Interaction rehearsal:**
1. Family shows the house. They say: "Ko te wharepaku kei kona."
2. They say: "Noho mai." → She sits.
3. She asks: "Kei hea te wharepaku?" (if needed)
4. She says: "Kei te hiamoe au" if tired.

**Content:**
```
"Tēnā, haere mai ki roto. Ko te wharepaku kei kona.
Noho mai. Kei te hiamoe koe? Kei te hiamoe au i te haerenga."
```

## Experience 5: Te kai me te inu — Food and Drink

**Scenario:** Family meal. Being offered food and drink.

**Entities:** THING (kai/food), THING (wai/water), THING (parāoa/bread), THING (miraka/milk), THING (ika/fish), ACTION (kai/eat), ACTION (inu/drink), STATE (hiakai/hungry), STATE (matewai/thirsty)

**Affordances:**
- person eats food → hunger satisfied
- person drinks water → thirst satisfied

**Phrases:**
| Priority | Phrase | English | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| MUST SAY | Kei te hiakai au | I am hungry | verified |
| MUST SAY | Kei te matewai au | I am thirsty | verified |
| MUST SAY | He wai, koa | Some water, please | unverified |
| MUST SAY | Kia ora mō te kai | Thank you for the food | verified |
| RECOGNIZE | He kai, koa | Some food, please | unverified |
| RECOGNIZE | Tēnā kōrua | Please help yourselves | unverified |
| CULTURAL | Kai | (offer of food, gesture) | — |

**Interaction rehearsal:**
1. Family member says: "Kai?" → She says "Āe" or "Kei te hiakai au."
2. They offer water. She says: "He wai, koa."
3. She eats. Afterwards: "Kia ora mō te kai."

**Content:**
```
"Kei te hiakai au. Kei te matewai au.
He wai, koa. He parāoa hoki.
Kia ora mō te kai. Ātaahua!"
```

## Experience 6: Te huarere — The Weather

**Scenario:** Looking outside. Commenting on the weather.

**Entities:** THING (ua/rain), THING (rā/sun), STATE (wera/hot), STATE (mātao/cold), STATE (makū/wet), ACTION (rain)

**Affordances:**
- sky rains
- sun shines
- weather makes hot/cold

**Phrases:**
| Priority | Phrase | English | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| MUST SAY | Kei te ua | It is raining | verified |
| RECOGNIZE | He rā paki | A fine day | unverified |
| RECOGNIZE | Kei te wera | It is hot | unverified |
| RECOGNIZE | Kei te mātao | It is cold | unverified |
| RECOGNIZE | Pēhea te huarere? | How is the weather? | unverified |

**Interaction rehearsal:**
1. Someone says: "Pēhea te huarere?" → She looks and says: "Kei te ua."
2. She says: "Kei te mātao au." (I am cold.)

**Content:**
```
"Pēhea te huarere? Kei te ua, engari he ātaahua tonu.
I Āwherika ki te Tonga, he wera. I konei, he mātao."
```

## Experience 12: Te poroporoaki — Goodbye

**Scenario:** Leaving. Saying goodbye to family.

**Entities:** ACTION (haere/go), ACTION (hoki/return), ACTION (tangī/miss), STATE (pōuri/sad)

**Affordances:**
- traveller departs
- family farewells

**Phrases:**
| Priority | Phrase | English | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| MUST SAY | Haere rā | Goodbye (said to those staying) | verified |
| MUST SAY | Ka hoki mai au | I will return | unverified |
| MUST SAY | Aroha nui | Much love | verified |
| RECOGNIZE | E noho rā | Goodbye (said to those leaving) | verified |
| RECOGNIZE | Ka tangī au | I will miss you / I will cry | unverified |
| CULTURAL | Kia kaha | Be strong (encouragement) | verified |

**Interaction rehearsal:**
1. She says: "Me haere au ināianei."
2. "Ka hoki mai au."
3. "Aroha nui ki a koutou katoa."
4. Family says: "Haere rā." She says: "E noho rā."

**Content:**
```
"Kua tae te wā. Me haere au ināianei.
Ka tangī au ki a koutou. Ka hoki mai au.
Haere rā, e te whānau. E noho rā.
Aroha nui ki a koutou katoa."
```
